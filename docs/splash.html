01 — Today vs Tomorrow

Where platform observability is heading

PayPal-scale commerce runs across global regions and clouds. The future is one analytics plane for logs, metrics, and traces: OpenTelemetry in, governed ingest, ML-aware detection, and AI-assisted response on Elastic Cloud Serverless so teams spend less time operating backends and more time improving merchant outcomes.

Legacy patternWhy it drives cost and risk
Fragmented stacks Logs, metrics, traces in separate products Correlating a merchant-impacting incident requires hopping tools; longer MTTR and duplicate licensing
Undifferentiated ingest Everything indexed, forever Storage and query costs grow with noise; on-call drowns in low-value alerts
Self-managed backends Capacity planning, upgrades, sharding Platform engineering time goes to plumbing, not product reliability
Weak cross-signal context Inconsistent IDs across signals Manual stitching across services; error-prone investigations at peak traffic
02 — Elastic Serverless + Lower TCO

Zero ops backend, usage-aligned economics

Elastic Cloud Serverless removes cluster lifecycle work and aligns spend with what you actually query and retain. Pair it with Elastic Streams to govern OpenTelemetry ingest (routing, retention, quality) so you index less noise and keep queryable history where it matters — that combination is how teams talk about lower TCO without giving up depth when incidents strike.

TCO in practice: Model ingest and retention tradeoffs with your team using the Observability TCO Comparison workshop companion (illustrative scenarios only). In the lab you will open Observability → Streams and tie stream choices directly to Serverless variable cost.

No cluster operations

Provision a complete Observability project in minutes. No shard sizing, JVM tuning, or rolling restarts on your plate.

Governed ingest (Streams)

Shape OTel pipelines before data accrues: fewer junk documents, clearer signals for alerts and ML, lower stored volume.

Native OTLP

Services send logs, metrics, and traces over OTLP. One wire format into a unified store and query model.

Elastic ML + ES|QL

Baseline anomalies on metrics and logs complement scheduled ES|QL rules — fewer brittle thresholds, faster MTTD.

Built-in AI Assistant

Investigate with an AI layer that stays inside your project boundary — no ad-hoc export to external chat tools.

Autonomous remediation

Serverless Workflows chain alert context, RCA, and controlled remediation hooks with an audit trail.

03 — OpenTelemetry as the shared language

Semantic conventions every team can rely on

The move forward is a vendor-neutral schema understood by collectors, backends, and CNCF tooling — not bespoke field dictionaries per system. OTel semantic conventions give you service, cloud, HTTP, and error semantics consistently across logs, metrics, and traces.

Typical legacy log fieldOTel semantic conventionWhat you gain
Service or app name (ad hoc)service.namePortable service identity; maps to APM and service maps automatically
Subsystem / domain prefixservice.namespaceCleaner grouping for large org charts and blast-radius views
Business transaction keytrace.id / span linksDistributed context across services without manual join keys
HTTP status or outcome codehttp.response.status_code, severity_textPortable error and SLO logic in ES|QL and dashboards
Wall-clock durationSpan duration and metric histogramsPercentiles and anomaly detection without one-off log parsers
Often missing in logs-only stackscloud.region, deployment.environmentInfrastructure context on every signal for capacity and incident views
04 — ES|QL at scale

One query language across logs (and metrics where mapped)

This workshop uses the Retail Banking Platform scenario in the lab VM. Examples below match the logs.otel paths and service names you will see in Kibana.

-- Errors by service (same pattern as lab workflows) FROM logs.otel, logs.otel.* | WHERE @timestamp > NOW() - 30 MINUTES AND severity_text == "ERROR" | STATS errors = COUNT(*) BY service.name | SORT errors DESC
-- Log volume over time by service (merchant-scale traffic shaping) FROM logs.otel, logs.otel.* | WHERE @timestamp > NOW() - 30 MINUTES | EVAL bucket5m = DATE_TRUNC(5 minutes, @timestamp) | STATS events = COUNT(*) BY bucket5m, service.name | SORT bucket5m DESC
-- Drill into a hot service (example: payment path) FROM logs.otel, logs.otel.* | WHERE @timestamp > NOW() - 15 MINUTES AND service.name == "payment-engine" | KEEP @timestamp, severity_text, body.text | SORT @timestamp DESC | LIMIT 25
